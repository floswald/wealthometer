5. The result

WHO WINS, WHO LOSES?

The first number indicates the increase in tax revenue to the federal government from your tax model. The second number tells you how much wealth tax you would have to pay yourself. The third number and the graphic illustrate what would happen if the additional tax revenues were paid out equally to all citizens.

Example: Suppose the exemption level is set to $50.000 and the tax rate above this amount is 1% per year. Assume that this policy yields an increase in per capita tax revenues of $651 per year. Imagine a household with wealth $90.000, which under our assumptions receives $651 in redistribution from the government and is liable for $400 per year. On net, this household is making a net gain of $251 per year. In this example, the lowest 75% in the wealth distribution realize a net gain. The top 25% experience a net loss from this policy.
