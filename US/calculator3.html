WHO WINS, WHO LOSES?

The first number displayed shows the tax revenue per capita given the tax parameters you proposed. The second number tells you how much wealth tax you would have to pay yourself. The third number and the graph illustrate what would happen if the additional tax revenues were distributed equally among all residents.

Example: Suppose the tax exempt amount is set to $50,000 and the tax rate above this amount is 1% per year. This policy yields per capita tax revenues of $XXX per year. Consider a household owning assets worth $90,000. Then this household receives $XXX from the redistribution of tax revenues, and owes taxes of $400 per yearleaving a net gain of $XXX per year. In this example, the lowest 75% in the wealth would gain from the proposed policy, while the top 25\% would lose.
